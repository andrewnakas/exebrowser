Play Age of Empires online in your browser Hit or miss — slow but sometimes playable

Age of Empires is the test case people most want answered: can a real RTS classic run in a browser tab? The honest answer is sometimes, and not at full speed. The original 1997 Age of Empires and Age of Empires II: The Age of Kings are 32-bit Win32 games from the right era, so they're plausible — but they lean on DirectDraw and DirectSound and are far more CPU-hungry than a card game, which is where browser-based emulation shows its limits. Expect to revisit a campaign mission or a small skirmish, not run a competitive late-game match.

Runs in your browser tab with WebAssembly + Wine — nothing is uploaded. Click the screen to capture input; press Esc to release the mouse.

Bring your own legally-owned copy. Age of Empires is commercial software published by Microsoft and is not free to redistribute, so we don't host it. Use an original disc-era install you own, or buy the modern Definitive Editions from the official Age of Empires site (note: the Definitive/HD editions are 64-bit Direct3D titles and will not run here). ExeBrowser is not affiliated with Microsoft or Ensemble Studios.

How it works & what to expect

What to expect from performance

You're emulating an x86 CPU inside WebAssembly, which delivers roughly 10–40% of native speed. The original AoE games ran fine on a late-90s Pentium, so a fast modern laptop has headroom — but a busy late-game map with hundreds of units doing pathfinding will feel sluggish, and audio can stutter. Small skirmishes, the campaign tutorial, and menus are the most playable. This is a "revisit it / show someone" experience, not a competitive-multiplayer one.

Load the full install folder, not just the EXE

AoE is not a single EXE — it needs its data, graphics, and sound files (the data, sound, and campaign folders plus various DLLs). Use the pick a folder or pick a zip option and upload the entire installed game directory so the executable finds everything beside it. Dropping just EMPIRES.EXE or AGE2_X1.EXE on its own will fail immediately.

If it won't start

  • Crash on launch? Try the r18 variant — its older engine sometimes starts games the default chokes on.
  • Black screen? Open the Console and look for err: lines about a missing DLL, then add that file to your folder.
  • Wrong resolution / tiny window? Some builds accept a windowed or resolution flag; the data-folder version is most forgiving.
  • The HD / Definitive editions won't work — they're modern 64-bit Direct3D titles outside what this engine supports. Use an original 1997/1999 disc-era install instead.

Frequently asked questions

Can I really play Age of Empires in a browser?

Sometimes. The original 1997 Age of Empires and AoE II (1999) are 32-bit Win32 games that can launch under Boxedwine, but they rely on DirectDraw and are CPU-heavy, so expect reduced speed and occasional audio stutter. Small skirmishes and campaign missions play best; large late-game battles will feel sluggish.

Why won't the HD or Definitive Edition run?

The HD and Definitive Editions are modern 64-bit games built on Direct3D. ExeBrowser only runs 32-bit Win32 software without DirectX 9+, so those remasters are outside what the engine supports. Use an original disc-era install of the 1997 or 1999 game.

Do you host Age of Empires for me to download?

No. Age of Empires is commercial software owned by Microsoft and we don't redistribute it. Bring your own legally-owned copy, or buy the official editions from ageofempires.com. We only host games that are free to redistribute.

Why do I need to upload the whole folder instead of just the EXE?

Age of Empires reads its data, sound, and campaign files from folders next to the executable. If you upload only EMPIRES.EXE or AGE2_X1.EXE, it can't find those assets and crashes on launch. Always upload the entire installed game directory as a folder or zip.

It crashes the moment it boots — what can I try?

Switch to the r18 Wine variant; its older engine sometimes starts games the default build chokes on. If you still get a black screen, open the Console and look for err: lines naming a missing DLL, then add that file to your uploaded folder.
