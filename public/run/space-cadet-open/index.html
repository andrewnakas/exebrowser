Play open-source Space Cadet pinball — instantly Free · open source · one-click

This is a fully open-source, instantly playable Space Cadet–style pinball game — no Microsoft files, no uploads, no install. It runs the open-source SpaceCadetPinball engine (MIT) compiled to WebAssembly, with brand-new original CC0 (public-domain) artwork and sound effects built from scratch. Click below and launch the ball in seconds.

Runs entirely in your browser with WebAssembly — nothing is uploaded. Click the game to capture input; press Esc to release. Launch with Space; flippers are Z and /. On mobile, use the FLIP / LAUNCH buttons below the table.

100% original & public domain. The table art, sounds, and data file are wholly original works dedicated to the public domain under CC0. This is not Microsoft's Space Cadet and contains none of its assets — it is an open, freely redistributable game inspired by it. The engine is MIT licensed. See the project notice for details.

How it works

Unlike most apps on ExeBrowser, this one isn't a Windows .exe running under Wine — it's a native WebAssembly build of an open-source pinball engine. The game's table graphics, physics geometry, and sound are packaged in a PINBALL.DAT data file plus a set of .wav sounds, all generated as original public-domain assets. Because nothing here is proprietary, the whole game can be hosted and redistributed freely.

Why it matters

The classic 3D Pinball for Windows – Space Cadet is proprietary Microsoft software, so historically you had to bring your own copy to play it online. This open build removes that requirement entirely: it's a clean-room, public-domain table on a community engine — the first version you can simply click and play with nothing to supply.

Controls

  1. Click ▶ Play Open-Source Space Cadet to load the game.
  2. Click the table to capture keyboard input.
  3. Hold Space to charge the plunger, release to launch the ball.
  4. Flip with Z (left) and / (right). Press Esc to release input.

Frequently asked questions

Is this the real Microsoft Space Cadet?

No — and that's the point. This is an independent, fully open-source pinball game inspired by Space Cadet. It runs the open-source SpaceCadetPinball engine (MIT) with brand-new, original public-domain (CC0) artwork and sound effects created from scratch. No Microsoft files are used or required.

Do I need to upload or download anything?

No. Everything is included and runs in your browser tab via WebAssembly. Click play and the table loads — no files to bring, no install, nothing uploaded to a server.

What are the controls?

Click the game to capture input. Press and hold Space to charge and launch the ball; flippers are Z (left) and / (right). Press Esc to release input back to the browser.

Can I reuse the game data?

Yes. The table data (PINBALL.DAT) and all sound effects are released under CC0 (public domain). The engine is MIT licensed. You are free to host, modify, and redistribute the whole thing.
